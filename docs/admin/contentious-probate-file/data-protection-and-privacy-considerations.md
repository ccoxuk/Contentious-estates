# Data Protection and Privacy Considerations - Contentious Probate

<!-- This document provides guidance on data protection and privacy compliance in contentious probate matters -->

## Purpose

To ensure compliance with data protection legislation (UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018) and maintain client confidentiality in contentious probate matters.

---

## Legal Framework

### Key Legislation

- **UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)**
- **Data Protection Act 2018**
- **Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003**
- **SRA Standards and Regulations** (confidentiality obligations)

### Regulatory Requirements

- **SRA Code of Conduct for Solicitors:** Duties of confidentiality
- **ICO Data Protection Guidance**
- **Legal professional privilege** considerations

---

## Personal Data in Contentious Probate

### What is Personal Data?

**Personal data means** information relating to an identified or identifiable living person.

### Types of Personal Data in Probate

**Standard Personal Data:**
- Client names and contact details
- Beneficiary details
- Witness information
- Party addresses and contact information
- Financial information about living persons

**Special Category Data (Sensitive):**
- Health information (capacity assessments, medical records)
- Genetic/biometric data
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Religious beliefs
- Details of family relationships

**Criminal Offence Data:**
- Allegations of fraud, forgery
- Undue influence involving coercion
- Criminal conduct by parties

### Data About Deceased Persons

**Not personal data under GDPR** (deceased have no data protection rights)

**BUT:**
- May reveal information about living persons (e.g., medical records)
- Duty of confidentiality may still apply
- Respect for deceased's privacy

---

## Lawful Basis for Processing

### For Client Data

**Primary lawful basis:**
- **Contract:** Processing necessary for performance of retainer
- **Legal obligation:** Compliance with legal/regulatory duties
- **Legitimate interests:** Where appropriate (rare in solicitor-client relationship)

**For special category data:**
- **Explicit consent** (usually)
- **Legal claims:** Necessary for establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims
- **Substantial public interest:** With appropriate safeguards

### For Third-Party Data

**Usually:**
- **Legitimate interests:** Subject to balancing test
- **Legal claims:** For litigation purposes
- **Consent:** Where appropriate and practical

---

## Data Protection Principles

### 1. Lawfulness, Fairness and Transparency

**Must:**
- Have lawful basis for processing
- Be transparent about how data is used
- Provide privacy notices
- Process fairly

**Actions:**
- [ ] Privacy notice provided to clients
- [ ] Explain data use in client care letter
- [ ] Obtain consent where required
- [ ] Document lawful basis

### 2. Purpose Limitation

**Must:**
- Collect data for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes
- Not process in manner incompatible with those purposes

**Actions:**
- [ ] Define purpose clearly (legal advice, litigation)
- [ ] Don't use for unrelated purposes without consent
- [ ] Limit access to those who need it

### 3. Data Minimisation

**Must:**
- Only collect data adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary

**Actions:**
- [ ] Only request necessary documents
- [ ] Redact irrelevant personal data where possible
- [ ] Don't collect excessive information
- [ ] Review data retention regularly

### 4. Accuracy

**Must:**
- Ensure data is accurate and kept up to date
- Correct or erase inaccurate data

**Actions:**
- [ ] Verify client details regularly
- [ ] Update records when changes notified
- [ ] Correct errors promptly
- [ ] Document source of information

### 5. Storage Limitation

**Must:**
- Keep data no longer than necessary
- Have retention policy

**Actions:**
- [ ] Follow firm's retention policy
- [ ] Contentious matters: typically 15 years from closure
- [ ] Review archived files periodically
- [ ] Securely destroy when retention expires

### 6. Integrity and Confidentiality (Security)

**Must:**
- Protect data with appropriate security measures
- Prevent unauthorized access, loss, destruction

**Actions:**
- [ ] Secure physical files (locked cabinets)
- [ ] Secure electronic files (access controls, encryption)
- [ ] Staff training on confidentiality
- [ ] Clear desk policy
- [ ] Secure disposal procedures

### 7. Accountability

**Must:**
- Be able to demonstrate compliance
- Maintain records of processing activities

**Actions:**
- [ ] Document data protection measures
- [ ] Record processing activities
- [ ] Conduct data protection impact assessments (if high risk)
- [ ] Regular compliance audits

## Data Subject Rights

### Right to be Informed

**What:** Privacy notice explaining data processing

**When:** At point of collecting data (client care letter)

**Content:**
- Identity of data controller (firm)
- Purpose of processing
- Lawful basis
- Recipients of data
- Retention period
- Data subject rights
- Right to complain to ICO

### Right of Access (Subject Access Request)

**What:** Right to obtain copy of personal data held

**Deadline:** One month from request

**Actions if SAR received:**
- [ ] Verify identity of requester
- [ ] Search all systems (paper and electronic)
- [ ] Redact privileged material
- [ ] Redact third-party personal data (if appropriate)
- [ ] Provide copy within deadline
- [ ] No fee (unless excessive/unfounded)
- [ ] Document compliance

**Exemptions:**
- Legal professional privilege
- Management forecasts
- Negotiations
- Confidential references

### Right to Rectification

**What:** Right to have inaccurate data corrected

**Deadline:** One month

**Actions:**
- [ ] Verify whether data is inaccurate
- [ ] Correct if inaccurate
- [ ] Inform anyone data was disclosed to
- [ ] Document correction

### Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

**What:** Right to have data deleted in certain circumstances

**Limited application** in litigation context

**Exemptions:**
- Compliance with legal obligation
- Establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims
- Necessary for purpose (ongoing litigation)

**Usually not applicable** while matter is active

### Right to Restrict Processing

**What:** Right to limit how data is used

**When:** Accuracy disputed, processing unlawful but erasure opposed, etc.

**Action:** Mark data as restricted, don't process except with consent or for legal claims

### Right to Data Portability

**Rarely applicable** in contentious probate context

### Right to Object

**What:** Right to object to processing based on legitimate interests

**Actions:**
- [ ] Assess objection
- [ ] If no compelling legitimate grounds, cease processing
- [ ] Usually doesn't apply where processing for legal claims

## Specific Issues in Contentious Probate

### Medical Records

**Special category data:** Requires extra protection

**Processing:**
- **Lawful basis:** Legal claims
- **Special category basis:** Explicit consent or legal claims
- **Obtain patient consent** where possible (from living persons featured)
- **Redact** irrelevant information about third parties

**Security:**
- Extra secure storage
- Limited access
- Encrypted if transmitted electronically

### Will Files from Third-Party Solicitors

**Contains personal data** of testator (deceased - no GDPR rights) and living persons

**Processing:**
- **Lawful basis:** Legal claims
- **Obtain under court order** or by consent
- **Privilege issues** may arise

### Witness Information

**Personal data** of living witnesses

**Processing:**
- **Lawful basis:** Legal claims
- **Inform witnesses** how data will be used (in proceedings, disclosed to opponent)
- **Obtain contact details** lawfully
- **Limit disclosure** to what's necessary

### Opponent/Third-Party Data

**Collect and use** only as necessary for litigation

**Disclose** only as required by disclosure obligations or court rules

**Don't use** for purposes unrelated to case (e.g., marketing)

### Data Shared with Court

**Court documents are public** (subject to some exceptions)

**Consider:**
- Anonymization applications (for sensitive data)
- Redaction of irrelevant personal data
- Confidentiality orders where appropriate

### Data Shared with Experts

**Processing:** Legal claims basis

**Requirements:**
- **Instruct expert** on confidentiality
- **Data sharing agreement** if appropriate
- **Limit data** to what expert needs
- **Secure transmission**
- **Require return/destruction** of data after case

## Security Measures

### Physical Security

- [ ] **Locked filing cabinets** for physical files
- [ ] **Secure office premises** with access control
- [ ] **Clear desk policy** (don't leave files visible)
- [ ] **Secure printing** (collect immediately, don't leave in printer)
- [ ] **Visitor management** (don't leave visitors alone with files)
- [ ] **Secure disposal** (shredding/confidential waste)

### Electronic Security

- [ ] **Access controls:** Role-based access, strong passwords
- [ ] **Encryption:** For files at rest and in transit
- [ ] **Firewalls and antivirus** software
- [ ] **Backup systems** (secure and encrypted)
- [ ] **Email security:** Encrypted email for sensitive data
- [ ] **Device security:** Laptops/mobiles encrypted, password-protected
- [ ] **Remote access:** Secure VPN only
- [ ] **Audit trails:** Log access to sensitive files

### Transmission Security

**Email:**
- [ ] Use encrypted email for sensitive data
- [ ] Double-check recipient addresses
- [ ] Mark as confidential/privileged
- [ ] Don't include sensitive data in subject line

**Post:**
- [ ] Use recorded/tracked delivery for important documents
- [ ] Mark envelopes "Private and Confidential"
- [ ] Don't send originals unless necessary

**Document Exchange:**
- [ ] Confirm correct DX number
- [ ] Mark as confidential

**Electronic File Sharing:**
- [ ] Use secure platforms (with encryption)
- [ ] Password-protect sensitive documents
- [ ] Audit trail of access

## Data Breaches

### What is a Data Breach?

**Any incident** leading to accidental or unlawful:
- Destruction, loss, alteration
- Unauthorized disclosure
- Unauthorized access to personal data

### Examples in Legal Practice

- Email sent to wrong recipient
- File left on train/in taxi
- Unauthorized access to case management system
- Loss/theft of laptop or device
- Hacking incident
- Improper disposal of documents

### Response to Data Breach

**Immediate actions (within 24 hours):**

1. **Contain the breach**
   - [ ] Retrieve document if possible
   - [ ] Disable compromised account
   - [ ] Isolate affected system

2. **Assess the breach**
   - [ ] What data was involved?
   - [ ] How many people affected?
   - [ ] What are the risks to individuals?
   - [ ] Can harm be mitigated?

3. **Notify internally**
   - [ ] Inform supervisor/partner immediately
   - [ ] Inform COLP/COFA
   - [ ] Inform IT/security team

4. **Consider notification obligations**
   
   **Notify ICO (within 72 hours) if:**
   - Breach likely to result in risk to individuals' rights and freedoms
   - Not if unlikely to result in risk (document decision)
   
   **Notify affected individuals if:**
   - Breach likely to result in high risk to their rights and freedoms
   - Without undue delay
   
   **Notify professional indemnity insurers** if potential claim

5. **Document the breach**
   - [ ] Record full details in breach register
   - [ ] Document decisions (notify or not)
   - [ ] Record remedial action

6. **Prevent recurrence**
   - [ ] Identify root cause
   - [ ] Implement corrective measures
   - [ ] Staff training if needed
   - [ ] Update procedures

## Staff Training and Awareness

### Training Requirements

**All staff must be trained on:**
- Data protection principles
- Confidentiality obligations
- Information security
- Recognizing and reporting breaches
- Subject access requests
- Clear desk policy

**Regular refresher training** (annually minimum)

### Confidentiality Agreements

- [ ] All staff sign confidentiality agreements
- [ ] Obligations continue after employment ends
- [ ] Specific training on legal professional privilege

---

## Third-Party Processors

### When Using Third Parties

**Examples:**
- Cloud storage providers
- Document management systems
- Counsel and experts
- Process servers
- Transcription services

**Requirements:**
- [ ] **Data processing agreement** in place
- [ ] **Security measures** adequate
- [ ] **Only process** on your instructions
- [ ] **Confidentiality** obligations
- [ ] **Notify** of any breaches
- [ ] **Return/delete** data when no longer needed

---

## International Transfers

### Transfers Outside UK

**If transferring data outside UK** (e.g., to expert abroad):

- [ ] **Check if adequate jurisdiction** (adequacy decision)
- [ ] If not adequate: **use appropriate safeguards**
  - Standard contractual clauses
  - Binding corporate rules
  - Certification schemes
- [ ] **Document transfer** and safeguards
- [ ] **Inform data subject**

**Usually rare** in domestic contentious probate

---

## Retention and Destruction

### Retention Periods

**Contentious files:** Usually **15 years** from closure (minimum 6 years)

**Considerations:**
- Ongoing limitation periods
- Potential for future claims/appeals
- Regulatory requirements
- Client instructions

### Secure Destruction

**When retention period expires:**

- [ ] **Review** whether still need to retain
- [ ] **Securely destroy** physical documents (shredding/confidential waste)
- [ ] **Securely delete** electronic data (proper deletion, not just to recycle bin)
- [ ] **Document destruction** in file closure record

---

## Compliance Checklist

### At Matter Opening

- [ ] Privacy notice provided to client
- [ ] Client care letter explains data use
- [ ] Consent obtained where needed
- [ ] Lawful basis documented
- [ ] Data minimisation considered

### During Matter

- [ ] Data securely stored (physical and electronic)
- [ ] Access limited to those who need it
- [ ] Data not used for unrelated purposes
- [ ] Regular file reviews for data accuracy
- [ ] Respond to data subject rights requests promptly

### At Matter Closure

- [ ] Retain per retention policy
- [ ] Securely archive
- [ ] Document retention date
- [ ] Review periodically
- [ ] Securely destroy when appropriate
