## Overview

Thorough case preparation is essential for successful contentious probate litigation. This section outlines the key steps from initial instruction through to case strategy formulation.

---

## Initial Client Instruction and Intake

### First Contact
- **Urgency Assessment**: Identify immediate deadlines or time-critical issues
- **Conflict Check**: Conduct preliminary conflict search
- **Initial Advice**: Provide high-level guidance without creating retainer
- **Fee Information**: Outline likely costs and funding options

### Initial Meeting/Consultation

**Objectives:**
1. Understand the client's concerns and objectives
2. Gather factual background
3. Identify key documents and evidence
4. Assess preliminary merits
5. Explain process, timescales, and costs

**Use Template:** [Initial Client Instruction Note](../templates/contentious-probate/template-initial-client-instruction-note.md)

### Information to Gather

#### About the Deceased
- Full name, date of birth, date of death
- Last known address
- Marital status and family circumstances
- Details of previous marriages/relationships
- Medical history (if capacity issue)
- Occupation and financial circumstances

#### About the Estate
- Approximate value and composition
- Real property details
- Bank accounts and investments
- Business interests
- Liabilities and debts
- Previous gifts and transfers

#### About the Will(s)
- Date of will in question
- Details of earlier wills
- Circumstances of will preparation and execution
- Solicitor who prepared the will
- Names of witnesses
- Location of original will

#### About the Dispute
- Nature of the challenge or claim
- Parties involved (beneficiaries, executors, etc.)
- Timeline of events
- Correspondence to date
- Any threats of litigation

## Preliminary Assessment and Due Diligence

### Limitation Analysis

**Use Checklist:** [Limitation and Deadlines Checklist](../templates/contentious-probate/checklist-limitation-and-deadlines.md)

- Identify applicable limitation period
- Calculate deadline dates
- Flag urgent matters requiring immediate action
- Diarize key dates in case management system

### Merits Assessment

**Evaluate:**
- **Strength of Legal Case**: Legal grounds and precedent
- **Quality of Evidence**: Available and obtainable evidence
- **Procedural Position**: Any procedural barriers or advantages
- **Commercial Reality**: Cost-benefit analysis
- **Alternative Remedies**: Other potential solutions

**Risk Grading:**
- Strong: >70% prospects of success
- Reasonable: 50-70% prospects
- Uncertain: 30-50% prospects
- Weak: <30% prospects

### Initial Document Collection

**Priority Documents:**
1. Death certificate
2. Will(s) - current and previous
3. Grant of probate/letters of administration (if issued)
4. Medical records (for capacity challenges)
5. Estate accounts and asset schedules
6. Correspondence between parties
7. Inheritance tax forms
8. Solicitor's will file (if available)

### Conflicts and Ethics Check

**Complete Comprehensive Conflict Search:**
- Deceased
- All potential parties
- Executors and administrators
- Beneficiaries under current and previous wills
- Professional advisers involved

**Consider:**
- Can you act for multiple parties?
- Information barriers needed?
- Informed consent requirements?
- Referral to another firm if conflict exists?

## Retainer and Costs

### Retainer Agreement

**Must Include:**
- Scope of work
- Fee structure and rates
- Payment terms
- Estimate of costs to key milestones
- Costs budgeting and management
- Complaints procedure
- Terms of business

**Funding Options:**
- Private funding
- Conditional Fee Agreements (CFAs)
- Damages-based agreements
- After-the-event insurance
- Legal expenses insurance
- Third-party funding

### Initial Costs Budget

Provide client with:
- Estimated costs to pre-action stage
- Estimated costs to issue of proceedings
- Estimated costs to trial/settlement
- Likely disbursements (counsel, experts, court fees)
- Costs risks and recoverable costs

## Initial Case Strategy and Planning

### Strategic Objectives

**Define:**
- Client's primary goals (financial, relational, reputational)
- Acceptable outcomes
- Red lines and deal-breakers
- Timeline preferences

### Case Theory Development

**Formulate:**
- Legal basis for claim/defense
- Key facts supporting position
- Anticipated counterarguments
- Evidence needed to prove case
- Weaknesses and how to address them

### Preliminary Tactical Decisions

**Consider:**
- Pre-action correspondence approach
- Timing of formal steps
- Disclosure strategy
- Expert evidence requirements
- Settlement prospects and timing
- ADR options (mediation, arbitration)

### Case Team and Resources

**Allocate:**
- Lead solicitor/fee earner
- Supervising partner
- Support staff
- Counsel (if appropriate)
- External experts (medical, handwriting, forensic accountants)

## Pre-Action Protocol Compliance

### Pre-Action Protocol for Inheritance Claims

Where applicable, comply with Practice Direction on Pre-Action Conduct and specific protocols:

1. **Letter of Claim**: Set out case clearly and concisely
2. **Response Deadline**: Allow reasonable time (typically 14-21 days minimum)
3. **Exchange of Information**: Provide sufficient detail to enable informed response
4. **Consider ADR**: Genuinely consider alternative dispute resolution
5. **Document Proportionality**: Keep pre-action costs proportionate
