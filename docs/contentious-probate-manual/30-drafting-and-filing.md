## General Drafting Principles

<!-- TODO: Expand drafting best practices -->

### Style and Format

<!-- TODO: Add detailed style guide -->

1. **Clarity and Precision**
   - Use plain English where possible
   - Define technical terms
   - Avoid unnecessary legalese
   - Be concise but complete

2. **Structure and Organization**
   - Logical flow of arguments
   - Numbered paragraphs
   - Clear headings and subheadings
   - Cross-references where appropriate

3. **Technical Requirements**
   - Font and formatting per CPR Practice Directions
   - Page numbering
   - Paragraph numbering
   - Proper citation of authorities

### Pleading Requirements

<!-- TODO: Add pleading rules specific to contentious probate -->

- Material facts must be pleaded
- No evidence in pleadings
- Particularity required for fraud, knowledge, condition of mind
- Ultimate facts, not evidence

### Particulars of Claim

Use [Particulars of Claim Skeleton Template](../templates/contentious-probate/template-particulars-of-claim-skeleton.md)

<!-- TODO: Add detailed drafting guidance for different claim types -->

#### Essential Elements

1. **Parties**
   - Identify all parties and their status
   - Explain their relationship to estate
   - State capacity (personal or representative)

2. **Background Facts**
   - Deceased's details and date of death
   - Details of will(s)
   - Grant of probate/letters of administration
   - Estate assets and value

3. **Factual Matrix**
   - Chronological narrative
   - Material facts supporting claim
   - Facts showing cause of action

4. **Legal Basis**
   - Statutory provisions relied upon
   - Common law principles
   - Particulars of breach/wrongdoing

5. **Loss and Damage**
   - Quantification of claim
   - Heads of damage
   - Calculation methodology

6. **Relief Sought**
   - Primary relief
   - Alternative relief
   - Interest
   - Costs

#### Claim-Specific Drafting

<!-- TODO: Add templates and guidance for each claim type -->

**Lack of Testamentary Capacity:**
- Plead Golden Rule breach if applicable
- Particularize incapacity
- Identify relevant time period
- Reference medical evidence

**Undue Influence:**
- Plead relationship of trust and confidence
- Particularize suspicious circumstances
- Identify alleged influencer
- Detail coercion or pressure

**Inheritance Act Claims:**
- Establish eligible claimant status
- Plead financial needs and resources
- Detail deceased's obligations and responsibilities
- Show insufficient provision

**Executor Misconduct:**
- Particularize breaches of fiduciary duty
- Detail losses caused
- Plead falsification or surcharge as appropriate
