## Overview

This section covers the drafting and filing of key documents in contentious probate litigation, from pre-action correspondence through to court applications and orders.

---

## Pre-Action Protocols and Correspondence

### Purpose of Pre-Action Correspondence

- Clarify issues in dispute
- Enable parties to assess merits
- Facilitate early settlement
- Reduce costs of litigation
- Comply with CPR Practice Direction on Pre-Action Conduct

### Initial Contact with Other Side

**Use Template:** [Initial Letter to Executor or Solicitor](../templates/contentious-probate/template-initial-letter-to-executor-or-solicitor.md)

**Objectives:**
- Introduce client and their interest
- Request relevant information and documents
- Set out preliminary concerns (if any)
- Maintain professional and constructive tone
- Preserve position without premature disclosure

### Letter Before Action

**Use Template:** [Letter Before Action - Contentious Probate](../templates/contentious-probate/template-letter-before-action-contentious-probate.md)

**Essential Content:**
1. **Parties**: Identify client and their relationship to estate
2. **Legal Basis**: State cause of action clearly
3. **Facts**: Provide concise chronology and relevant facts
4. **Case**: Summarize legal and factual basis for claim
5. **Relief Sought**: Specify what client seeks
6. **Documents**: List key documents relied upon
7. **ADR**: Propose alternative dispute resolution
8. **Response Deadline**: Specify reasonable time for response (typically 14-21 days)
9. **Next Steps**: State intention to issue proceedings if no satisfactory response

**Tone and Approach:**
- Professional and measured
- Clear and unambiguous
- Avoid inflammatory language
- Preserve negotiation possibilities
- Protect client's position

## Drafting Pleadings and Statements of Case

### General Principles

**CPR Requirements:**
- Contain concise statement of facts relied upon
- Specify remedy sought
- Verified by statement of truth
- Comply with relevant practice directions

**Drafting Style:**
- Clear and concise language
- Short numbered paragraphs
- Chronological where appropriate
- Focus on material facts, not evidence
- Avoid unnecessary legal argument

### Particulars of Claim

**Use Template:** [Particulars of Claim Skeleton](../templates/contentious-probate/template-particulars-of-claim-skeleton.md)

**Structure:**
1. **Introduction**: Parties and their status
2. **The Deceased**: Relevant details
3. **The Will/Estate**: Subject matter of dispute
4. **Background Facts**: Chronological narrative
5. **Cause of Action**: Legal grounds for claim
6. **Particulars**: Specific allegations (capacity, undue influence, etc.)
7. **Alternative Claims**: If applicable
8. **Relief Sought**: Specific remedies
9. **Interest**: Claim for interest if applicable
10. **Statement of Truth**: Required verification

**Common Contentious Probate Claims:**

#### Lack of Testamentary Capacity
- Testator's age and health
- Medical evidence of incapacity
- Circumstances of will preparation
- Application of *Banks v Goodfellow* test

#### Want of Knowledge and Approval
- Suspicious circumstances
- Testator's understanding compromised
- Unusual provisions requiring explanation
- Burden of proof issues

#### Undue Influence
- Relationship between influencer and testator
- Vulnerability of testator
- Coercion or pressure alleged
- Effect on will provisions

#### Inheritance Act 1975 Claims
- Claimant's status (spouse, child, dependant)
- Financial resources and needs
- Obligations and responsibilities
- Size and nature of estate
- Reasonable provision sought

### Defence and Counterclaim

**Structure:**
1. **Admissions**: Facts admitted
2. **Denials**: Facts denied with reasons
3. **Non-Admissions**: Facts neither admitted nor denied
4. **Positive Case**: Defendant's version of events
5. **Counterclaim**: If applicable
6. **Relief Sought**: By way of counterclaim

**Defensive Strategies:**
- Deny fundamental allegations
- Plead affirmative defenses
- Request strict proof
- Challenge claimant's standing or limitation
- Assert procedural defects

### Reply

- Respond to new matters in Defence
- Do not repeat Particulars of Claim
- Keep focused and concise
- Address defences specifically

## Witness Statements

**Use Template:** [Witness Statement Skeleton](../templates/contentious-probate/template-witness-statement-skeleton.md)

### Purpose and Function

- Present evidence in written form
- Stand as evidence-in-chief at trial
- Comply with CPR Part 32 and Practice Direction 32

### Format and Requirements

**Heading:**
- Case title and number
- Name of witness
- Statement number (if multiple statements)
- Exhibits (if any)
- Date

**Content:**
1. **Introduction**: Who the witness is
2. **Background**: Witness's connection to matter
3. **Chronological Account**: Factual narrative
4. **Documents**: Reference to key documents
5. **Opinion**: Only if expert witness
6. **Truth**: Statement of truth

**Drafting Guidelines:**
- First person ("I saw...", "I heard...")
- Witness's own words and style
- Clear and specific
- Avoid hearsay where possible
- Exhibit documents correctly
- Number paragraphs consecutively
- Include statement of truth

### Exhibits

**Format:**
- Mark as "[Initials]-[Number]" (e.g., "JAS-1")
- Exhibit page with formal wording
- Paginate consecutively
- Index if multiple documents

## Evidence and Disclosure

### Standard Disclosure

**Obligations:**
- Documents on which party relies
- Documents that adversely affect own case
- Documents that adversely affect another party's case
- Documents that support another party's case

### Contentious Probate Specific Evidence

**Medical Evidence:**
- GP records
- Hospital records
- Mental capacity assessments
- Expert medical opinions

**Documentary Evidence:**
- Will file from solicitor
- Attendance notes
- Correspondence
- Financial records
- Photographs, videos

**Expert Evidence:**
- Handwriting experts
- Medical experts (psychiatrists, geriatricians)
- Forensic accountants
- Professional negligence experts

**Use Checklist:** [Disclosure and Evidence Checklist](../templates/contentious-probate/checklist-disclosure-and-evidence.md)

## Court Applications and Orders

### Interim Applications

**Common Applications:**
- Extend time for serving defence
- Summary judgment
- Strike out
- Disclosure orders
- Freezing injunctions
- Interim injunctions
- Substitution/removal of parties

**Application Notice:**
- Form N244
- Statement in support
- Draft order
- Evidence
- Fee

### Consent Orders

**Use Template:** [Consent Order or Tomlin Order Heads](../templates/contentious-probate/template-consent-order-or-tomlin-order-heads.md)

**Types:**
- **Simple Consent Order**: Settlement terms incorporated
- **Tomlin Order**: Stay with confidential schedule

**Requirements:**
- Agreed by all parties
- Signed by legal representatives
- Court approval (may require hearing)
- Provides for costs

## Issuing Proceedings

### Claim Form

**CPR Part 7 or Part 8:**
- **Part 7**: Standard procedure for substantial disputes
- **Part 8**: Alternative procedure for limited disputes or interpretation issues

**Court Selection:**
- High Court (Chancery Division) - most contentious probate
- County Court - lower value claims
- Specialist probate registries

### Filing Requirements

**Documents:**
- Claim form (N1 or N208)
- Particulars of claim
- Fee or exemption application
- Schedule of costs (if summary judgment sought)

**Service:**
- Serve on all defendants
- 4 months for service within jurisdiction
- Different rules for service out of jurisdiction

## Statements of Case - Amendments

### When Amendment Needed

- Correct errors
- Add new parties
- Plead alternative cases
- Respond to developments

### Permission Requirements

- Before service: no permission needed
- After service, before close of pleadings: consent or permission
- After close of pleadings: court permission

**Late Amendments:**
- Generally discouraged
- Must explain delay
- May affect costs
