## Primary Legislation

### Wills and Testamentary Capacity

**Wills Act 1837**
- Section 9: Requirements for valid execution of wills
- Section 11: Wills made by soldiers and mariners
- Section 18: Effect of marriage on wills
- Section 18A: Effect of dissolution or annulment of marriage
- Section 20: Revocation of wills

**Mental Capacity Act 2005**
- Sections 1-3: Principles and definition of capacity
- Sections 9-14: Lasting powers of attorney
- Section 18: Statutory wills
- Schedule A1: Deprivation of liberty safeguards

### Estate Administration

**Administration of Estates Act 1925**
- Part I: Devolution of real and personal estate
- Part II: Administration of real and personal estate
- Part III: Administration of insolvent estates
- Section 46: Succession to real and personal estate on intestacy
- Section 47: Statutory trusts in favour of issue and other classes of relatives
- Section 55: General administration powers

**Senior Courts Act 1981**
- Section 25: Probate jurisdiction
- Section 50: Power to award interest
- Section 114: Probate actions
- Section 116: Applications for grant of probate or administration

**Trustee Act 1925 and Trustee Act 2000**
- Trustee duties and powers
- Investment powers
- Delegation provisions
- Appointment and removal of trustees

### Family Provision

**Inheritance (Provision for Family and Dependants) Act 1975**
- Section 1: Application for financial provision from deceased's estate
- Section 2: Powers of court to make orders
- Section 3: Matters to which court is to have regard
- Section 4: Time limit for applications
- Section 10: Interim orders
- Section 11: Variation of orders

**Practice Directions**

## Leading Case Law

### Testamentary Capacity

**Banks v Goodfellow (1870) LR 5 QB 549**
- The definitive test for testamentary capacity
- Four-part test still applied today

**Perrins v Holland [2010] EWCA Civ 840**
- Modern application of Banks v Goodfellow
- Medical evidence and capacity assessment

**Walker v Badmin [2014] EWHC 71 (Ch)**
- Capacity and the "golden rule"
- Role of medical evidence

**James v James [2018] EWHC 43 (Ch)**
- Recent guidance on testamentary capacity
- Weight of medical evidence

### Knowledge and Approval

**Fulton v Andrew (1875) LR 7 HL 448**
- Burden of proof in suspicious circumstances
- When court requires affirmative proof

**Gill v Woodall [2010] EWCA Civ 1430**
- Knowledge and approval in modern context
- Role of professional will-drafters

**Hoff v Atherton [2004] EWCA Civ 1554**
- Suspicious circumstances doctrine
- Burden of proof shifting

### Undue Influence

**Hall v Hall (1868) LR 1 P&D 481**
- Classic definition of undue influence in probate context

**Edwards v Edwards [2007] EWHC 1119 (Ch)**
- Modern approach to undue influence claims
- Standard and burden of proof

**Schrader v Schrader [2013] EWHC 466 (Ch)**
- Recent guidance on undue influence
- Vulnerability and coercion

### Want of Due Execution

**Wright v Rogers [1869] LR 1 P&D 678**
- Requirements for attestation
- Witness must observe signature

**Wood v Smith [1993] Ch 90**
- Acknowledgment of signature before witnesses
- Compliance with s.9 Wills Act 1837

**Sherrington v Sherrington [2005] EWCA Civ 326**
- Modern application of execution requirements
- Substantial compliance doctrine

### Inheritance Act 1975 Claims

**Ilott v Mitson [2017] UKSC 17**
- Leading Supreme Court case on adult child claims
- Reasonable financial provision

**Re Coventry [1980] Ch 461**
- Maintenance standard vs surviving spouse standard
- Different categories of claimants

**Nahajec v Fowle [2017] EWHC 1607 (Ch)**
- Recent guidance on reasonable provision
- Adult child dependency

**Miles v Shearer [2021] EWHC 1000 (Ch)**
- Modern approach to discretion under 1975 Act
- Section 3 factors analysis

### Proprietary Estoppel

**Thorner v Major [2009] UKHL 18**
- Proprietary estoppel in inheritance context
- Clear and unambiguous assurance required

**Davies v Davies [2016] EWCA Civ 463**
- Proprietary estoppel claims
- Detrimental reliance

**Habberfield v Habberfield [2019] EWCA Civ 890**
- Recent Court of Appeal guidance
- Proportionality of remedy

### Executor and Administrator Duties

**Letterstedt v Broers (1884) 9 App Cas 371**
- Removal of executors and trustees
- Welfare of beneficiaries paramount

**Re Beloved Wilkes' Charity (1851) 3 Mac & G 440**
- Standard required of trustees and executors
- Ordinary prudent man test

**Armitage v Nurse [1998] Ch 241**
- Trustee liability and exemption clauses
- Irreducible core of trustee obligations
