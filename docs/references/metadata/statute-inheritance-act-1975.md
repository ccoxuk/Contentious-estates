# Inheritance (Provision for Family and Dependants) Act 1975

This Act is the primary legislation for family provision claims and contentious probate matters
in England and Wales.

## Key Sections

- **Section 1**: Applications for financial provision from deceased's estate
- **Section 2**: Powers of court to make orders
- **Section 3**: Matters to which court is to have regard in exercising powers
- **Section 4**: Time limit for applications

## Categories of Applicants

The Act allows applications by:

1. The spouse or civil partner of the deceased
2. A former spouse or civil partner who has not remarried
3. A child of the deceased
4. Any person treated by the deceased as a child of the family
5. Any person being maintained by the deceased immediately before death
6. Any person who cohabited with the deceased for at least 2 years before death

## Standard of Provision

The Act distinguishes between:

- **Surviving spouse/civil partner**: Such financial provision as would be reasonable in all
  circumstances (the matrimonial standard)
- **Other applicants**: Such financial provision as would be reasonable for maintenance
